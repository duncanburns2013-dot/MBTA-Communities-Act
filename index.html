The Revolving Door

Three consecutive Housing Secretaries — across the Patrick and Baker administrations — left government for the development industry:

Gregory Bialecki PATRICK ADMIN 2009–2015
→ Redgate, a RE development firm. His work “helped pave the way for the MBTA Communities Act.”
Sources: PRNewswire · Wikipedia · MassINC
Jay Ash BAKER ADMIN 2015–2018
→ CEO of MACP, whose board includes John Fish, CEO of Suffolk Construction ($2.3B).
Sources: BusinessWire · MACP
Mike Kennealy BAKER ADMIN 2018–2022
→ Running for governor to “fix” the law he helped craft. Self-funded ~$2M exceeding $200K/cycle state limit. OCPF demanded reclassification; his DC lawyer refused.
Sources: Herald Jun 2025 · Herald Oct 2025
Brian Shortsleeve BAKER’S MBTA CHIEF
→ Running to repeal § 3A — but donated $2,000 to AG Campbell, who enforces it against non-compliant towns.
Sources: Boston.com · OCPF filings

The $4.4 Billion Question

A law that mandates zoning but not construction, requires no affordable units, shifts billions in costs to municipalities, strips local control, and operates in a frozen market — does not solve a housing affordability crisis.

It does accomplish a permanent transfer of zoning authority from 177 towns to the state.

The question is whether that was the purpose all along.
