03.1
Who Pays vs. Who Profits

Towns Pay

Rezoning consultants, planning staff, infrastructure (water/sewer/roads/schools), legal fees, environmental review, compliance monitoring. The state mandated it all and sent $0.

Developers Collect

As-of-right zoning: no special permits, no town votes. Market-rate units: zero affordability. Then they donate $600K+ back to the officials who keep the cycle running.

07
The $4.4 Billion Question

A law that mandates zoning but not construction, requires no affordable units, shifts billions in costs to municipalities, strips local control, and operates in a frozen market — does not solve a housing affordability crisis.

It does accomplish a permanent transfer of zoning authority from 177 towns to the state.

The question is whether that was the purpose all along.

The Pattern

A governor signs a housing mandate → leaves office, joins a lobbying firm. His housing secretary joins a developer. His MBTA chief runs for governor pledging repeal — while donating to the AG who enforces it. A $1.9B foundation funds compliance, then its leaders donate to enforcers. 103 donors fund both sides. One dynasty gives $100K+ to everyone.
